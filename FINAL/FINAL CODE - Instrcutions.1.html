Golf Terms Every Beginner Should Know:

  • Fairway

    The fairway is the short, neatly mowed area between the tee box and the green. Landing here gives you a clean lie and an easier next shot.

  • Rough

    The rough is the longer, thicker grass beside the fairway. Shots from the rough are tougher because the grass grabs the clubface and ball.

  • Tee Box

    The flat starting area of each hole where you place your ball on a tee for the first shot. Different tee markers indicate varying difficulty or yardage.

  • Bunker

    A hollow filled with sand, also called a sand trap. Getting out typically requires an open clubface and a splash of sand that lifts the ball out.

  • Green

    The smooth, closely‑mown grass surrounding the hole. Here you switch to the putter and focus on pace and line to sink the ball.

  • Handicap

    A numerical measure of a player’s potential ability. It levels the playing field by allowing weaker players extra strokes against stronger ones.

  • Par

    The number of strokes an expert golfer is expected to need to complete a hole (or entire course). Holes are usually par‑3, par‑4, or par‑5.

  • Birdie, Eagle, Albatross

    Scoring terms for finishing a hole in fewer strokes than par: Birdie = 1 under par, Eagle = 2 under, Albatross (double eagle) = 3 under.

  • Bogey, Double Bogey, Triple Bogey

    Finishing a hole over par: Bogey = 1 over, Double Bogey = 2 over, Triple Bogey = 3 over par.
